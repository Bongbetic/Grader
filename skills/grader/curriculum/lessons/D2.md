# Context & Motivation

Context is not filler. It tells the model who the output is for, what situation it is entering, and what constraints are real. Motivation explains why those constraints matter.

When you share the audience and the purpose, the model can resolve edge cases you never wrote out. Without it, you get generic output that misses the reader.

- Try this: Add one sentence: "The reader is an existing customer who values reliability over hype; the goal is a single click to the product page."
